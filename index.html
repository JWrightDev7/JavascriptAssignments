Chad Kroeger

  • Chad Kroeger is a Canadian singer, songwriter, guitarist, and producer. He is best known as the lead singer and guitarist of the band Nickelback. He was born on November 15th, 1974 as Chad Robert Turton, after his father left when he was 2 years of age he started to go by his mothers maiden name of Kroeger. He then learned to play guitar at the age of 13.
  • In the 1990s Chad was part of a grunge cover band called Village Idiot, with his cousin Brandon Kroeger and future Nickelback members including his brother Mike Kroeger and singer Ryan Peake. In 1995 they all came together to form the band Nickelback. Kroeger has continued to tour and record with the band for more than 20 years. In 2001 Chad Kroeger founded the band 'The Suits XL' band members included Sam Loubier-Demers and Yohann Gosselin, Patrick Paquet, Felix0Antoine Berube and finally Oliver Roy de Belleval. The following year he co-founded the Vancouver-based recording label 604 Records. Later on in 2006 'The Suits XL' released an album through 604 Records, the next year the band was nominated for a Canadian Indie Award.
    • Bands such as Default, Theory of a Deadman and Bo Bice all had music produced by Chad.
    • Kroeger, Josey Scott of Saliva, Tyler Connolly of Theory of a Deadman and Jeremy Taggart all performed the song 'Hero', the theme song for the hit movie Spiderman(2002).
    • Also in 2002 Chad sang on "Why Don't You & I" for Santana's Shaman Album.
  • In 2008, Kroeger was conviced of drunk driving in British Columbia after he was stopped for speeding. He started dating fellow Canadian Avril Lavigne on July 1st, 2012. In March of 2012 the two started to write and record songs together for Avril's 5th album and later got married in the south of France in 2013. On September 2nd, 2015 Avril announced that they had separated.
  • Singles include only one song, 'Hero'. As a featured artist Chad was a part of a few songs including "Into the Night" by Santana, "Let Me Go" By Avril Lavigne, and two others. Chad was also a part of all of Nickelback's Albums including Curb, The State, Silver Side Up, The Long Road, All The Right Reasons and 4 others.
